## The Master Portfolio & Execution Order
### PHASE 1: The Factory (The "God Boilerplate" Architecture)
Before writing a single line of business logic for any product, the manufacturing line must be built. Operating on your Hetzner VPS with massive overhead (AMD EPYC, 16GB RAM) means you can host Kelaro, your personal site, and dozens of these new SaaS utilities simultaneously—if they are containerized correctly.
- **The Standalone Core:** A standardized Next.js (App Router) template explicitly configured for `output: 'standalone'` to keep Docker images under 150MB, preserving server RAM.
- **The Observation Layer:** A custom logging wrapper (`logger.ts`) baked into the boilerplate so every new app automatically feeds perfectly formatted logs into your Promtail/Grafana Command Center with zero extra configuration.
- **The Traffic Cop:** A reverse proxy (Nginx) to securely route traffic to `kelaro.com`, your personal sites, and the specific utility domains.
- **The Heavy Lifter (PDF Queue):** Generating legally compliant PDFs (especially with embedded photos) is the biggest threat to server RAM. The boilerplate must include a strict Redis + BullMQ queue worker to isolate this load.
- **The Outbound Engine:** A Python background worker that scrapes Portuguese Google Maps directories (real estate agencies, restaurants, transport fleets) to feed automated cold outreach campaigns.

### PHASE 3: The Assembly Line (Execution & Endgame)
With the factory built and the target selected, execution begins. The strategy here relies on ruthless efficiency and risk mitigation.
- **Liability Avoidance:** You are strictly avoiding financial escrow, complex API weather forecasting, and legal concierge services. You only build tools where the worst-case scenario is a minor inconvenience (a PDF takes 10 extra seconds to load), never a financial disaster or lawsuit.    
- **The Context-Switching Trap:** Focus marketing efforts on just one or two apps at a time before expanding.
- **Ruthless Pruning:** If an app is live for 60 days and generates zero MRR despite targeted AI outreach, kill it instantly. Double down only on what the market actually pays for.
- **The Revenue Endgame:** You are weaponizing AI to build unsexy, highly specific digital scalpels for the boring, paper-driven workflows of the Portuguese economy. Global software giants ignore these localized networks because the TAM is "too small." But for a solo operator running a 30€/month Hetzner server, it is a goldmine. Capturing just 5% to 10% of these niches points to a combined **~28,000€ to 38,000€ MRR** (roughly 350,000€ to 450,000€ ARR). Combine that passive, high-margin utility income with a remote job salary and Kelaro's projected MRR, and you engineer absolute financial sovereignty.
